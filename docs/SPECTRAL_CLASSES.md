# Asteroid Spectral Classes and Resource Mapping

## Overview

Helios Ascension uses scientifically accurate spectral classification for asteroids based on real astronomical data from NASA, JPL, and the asteroid research community. This document explains how each spectral class maps to in-game resources.

## Scientific Basis

### Primary Sources

1. **JPL Small-Body Database (SBDB)**
   - URL: https://ssd.jpl.nasa.gov/sbdb.cgi
   - Provides physical parameters and taxonomy for all cataloged asteroids

2. **Asterank Economic Database**
   - URL: http://www.asterank.com/
   - Calculates economic value based on spectral types
   - Open source: https://github.com/typpo/asterank

3. **Bus-DeMeo Taxonomy System**
   - DeMeo et al. (2009): "The compositional structure of the asteroid belt"
   - Standard classification system used by professional astronomers

4. **NASA Planetary Fact Sheet**
   - URL: https://nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov/planetary/factsheet/
   - Reference values for physical parameters

## Spectral Classes

### C-Type: Carbonaceous (75% of asteroids)

**Scientific Description:**
- Darkest asteroids with very low albedo (~0.03-0.07)
- Carbon-rich composition with hydrated minerals
- Found throughout the belt, dominate outer regions
- Primitive composition from early solar system

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Phyllosilicates (clay minerals)
- Carbonates
- Organic compounds
- Water ice (up to 20% by mass)
- Magnetite

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Water | 10-45% | Higher beyond frost line |
| Hydrogen | 8-15% | From hydrated minerals |
| Ammonia | 5-12% | Primitive volatiles |
| Methane | 3-8% | Organic compounds |
| CO2 | 4-10% | Carbonates |
| Iron | 5-15% | Low but present |
| Silicates | 15-25% | Phyllosilicates |

**Real Examples:**
- 1 Ceres (largest asteroid, now dwarf planet)
- 10 Hygiea
- 24 Themis (water ice confirmed via spectroscopy)

**Strategic Value:** Excellent water sources, ideal for fuel/life support in asteroid belt

---

### S-Type: Silicaceous (17% of asteroids)

**Scientific Description:**
- Moderate albedo (~0.10-0.22)
- Stony composition, undifferentiated
- Dominant in inner asteroid belt (<2.5 AU)
- Composed of olivine and pyroxene

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Olivine ((Mg,Fe)₂SiO₄)
- Pyroxene ((Ca,Mg,Fe)SiO₃)
- Metallic iron-nickel (5-15%)
- Plagioclase feldspar

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Silicates | 35-50% | Dominant component |
| Iron | 20-35% | From metal inclusions |
| Aluminum | 8-15% | From feldspars |
| Copper | 0.02-0.08% | Trace metals |
| Rare Earths | 0.01-0.04% | Present in minerals |
| Water | 0-8% | Only if beyond frost line |

**Real Examples:**
- 8 Flora
- 3 Juno
- 433 Eros (NEAR Shoemaker landing site)

**Strategic Value:** Best source of construction materials (iron, silicates) in inner belt

---

### M-Type: Metallic (8% of asteroids)

**Scientific Description:**
- High albedo (~0.10-0.30)
- Almost pure nickel-iron metal
- Remnant cores of differentiated planetesimals
- Most valuable for mining

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Iron-nickel metal (70-95%)
- Kamacite (low-Ni iron)
- Taenite (high-Ni iron)
- Platinum Group Metals (PGM) enriched
- Schreibersite (Fe,Ni)₃P

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Iron | 70-85% | Dominant metal |
| Platinum | 0.00001-0.0001% | 10-20x crustal average |
| Gold | 0.000005-0.00005% | Elevated |
| Silver | 0.00001-0.00008% | Elevated |
| Copper | 0.1-0.5% | Higher than crust |
| Rare Earths | 0.02-0.1% | Concentrated |
| Silicates | 2-8% | Minimal |

**Real Examples:**
- 16 Psyche (target of NASA mission, may be exposed core)
- 21 Lutetia
- 216 Kleopatra (dog-bone shaped)

**Strategic Value:** HIGHEST VALUE - precious metals at 10-20x normal abundance!

---

### V-Type: Vestoid (Rare <1%)

**Scientific Description:**
- Basaltic composition
- Fragments from 4 Vesta's differentiated crust
- High albedo (~0.30-0.40)
- Formed through igneous processes

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Basaltic achondrite material
- Pyroxene-rich
- Plagioclase feldspar
- High calcium content
- Titanium-rich minerals (ilmenite)

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Silicates | 40-55% | Basaltic minerals |
| Titanium | 2-5% | Unusually high! |
| Iron | 15-28% | Moderate |
| Aluminum | 10-18% | From feldspars |
| Copper | 0.01-0.05% | Trace |

**Real Examples:**
- 4 Vesta (parent body, 2nd largest asteroid)
- 1929 Kollaa
- 2851 Harbin

**Strategic Value:** Best source of titanium for high-strength applications

---

### D-Type: Dark Primitive (Outer Belt/Trojans)

**Scientific Description:**
- Extremely dark (albedo <0.05)
- Very red spectrum
- Found beyond 3.5 AU
- Most primitive composition
- Jupiter Trojan asteroids

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Organic-rich material
- Water ice (>30%)
- Ammonia ice
- Methane clathrates
- Silicates embedded in ice matrix
- Anhydrous (never heated)

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Water | 35-55% | Extremely high! |
| Methane | 15-30% | Clathrate form |
| Ammonia | 12-25% | Ammonia ice |
| Hydrogen | 10-20% | Bound in organics |
| Nitrogen | 5-12% | Nitrogen compounds |
| Silicates | 5-15% | Low, in matrix |
| Iron | 2-8% | Very low |

**Real Examples:**
- 624 Hektor (largest Jupiter Trojan)
- 617 Patroclus (binary system)
- Most Jupiter Trojans

**Strategic Value:** Water and volatiles "oases" in outer solar system - perfect for refueling stations

---

### P-Type: Primitive (Outer Belt)

**Scientific Description:**
- Low albedo (~0.02-0.06)
- Similar to D-type but less red
- Found in outer main belt
- Primitive, low-temperature formation

**Composition (Scientific):**
- Similar to D-type
- Slightly less organic material
- High water ice content
- Ammonia-bearing minerals
- Anhydrous silicates

**Game Resource Mapping:**
| Resource | Abundance Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Water | 30-48% | Very high |
| Ammonia | 10-22% | High |
| Methane | 12-25% | High |
| Hydrogen | 8-18% | Elevated |
| CO2 | 6-15% | Present |
| Silicates | 8-18% | Moderate |
| Iron | 3-10% | Low |

**Real Examples:**
- 87 Sylvia (triple system with two moons)
- 107 Camilla
- 46 Hestia

**Strategic Value:** Similar to D-type, excellent volatile source in outer belt

## Distribution in Solar System

### By Distance from Sun

| Distance (AU) | Dominant Type | Secondary Types | Reason |
|---------------|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| 2.0-2.5 | S-Type | C-Type | Inner belt, warm, rocky |
| 2.5-2.8 | S/C Mix | M-Type (rare) | Transition zone |
| 2.8-3.5 | C-Type | S-Type, M-Type | Outer belt, cooler |
| 3.5-5.2 | C-Type, D/P-Type | - | Very cold, primitive |
| 5.2 (Trojans) | D-Type | P-Type | Jupiter Trojans |

| D-Type | Varies | Jupiter Trojans |
| P-Type | Varies | Outer belt |

## Economic Implications

### Resource Value Ranking (per unit mass)

1. **M-Type** - Platinum group metals, gold (extreme value)
2. **V-Type** - Titanium for advanced construction
3. **S-Type** - Iron and silicates (high volume, moderate value)
4. **D/P-Type** - Volatiles in outer system (strategic value)
5. **C-Type** - Water in inner belt (moderate value)

### Mining Strategy

**Early Game (Inner System):**
- Target S-Type for construction materials
- Seek C-Type for water/fuel

**Mid Game (Main Belt):**
- Hunt for rare M-Type asteroids (jackpot!)
- Mine V-Type for titanium
- C-Type for volatiles

**Late Game (Outer System):**
- D/P-Type for refueling stations
- Massive volatile reserves
- Enable deep space missions

## Implementation Notes

### In-Game Detection

Spectral class can be determined by:
1. **Database lookup** - Real asteroids have known classes
2. **Spectral analysis** - Survey missions can classify unknown asteroids
3. **Procedural generation** - Generated asteroids assigned class based on:
   - Distance from sun
   - Statistical distribution
   - Parent body (if fragment)

### Resource Generation Priority

1. Special body profiles (Europa, Mars, etc.) - HIGHEST
2. Spectral class profiles (if known) - HIGH
3. Random specialization (15% chance) - MEDIUM
4. Distance-based generation (fallback) - BASELINE

### Combining Systems

An M-Type asteroid with the "Platinum-Rich" specialization would have:
- Base M-Type: Iron 75%, Platinum 0.00005%
- Specialization: Platinum × 250 = 0.0125% (1.25%!)
- Result: Jackpot asteroid worth billions!
